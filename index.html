Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Jaipur Palace

The Jaipur Palace, also known as the City Palace, is a historic royal palace in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan. It includes several palatial structures and a museum displaying artifacts from the royal family.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in Delhi, India. It was the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal dynasty for over 200 years. The fort is known for its stunning red sandstone architecture.

Hampi Ruins

Hampi, located in Karnataka, is an ancient city known for its ruins and historical monuments. It was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a minaret in Delhi, India, and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is one of the tallest minarets in the world and is a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Khajuraho Group of Monuments

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments in Madhya Pradesh is famous for its intricate erotic sculptures and stunning temple architecture, dating back to the 10th century.

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace in Karnataka is a magnificent royal residence and a symbol of the city's rich history. It features a blend of Indo-Saracenic, Rajput, and Gothic architectural styles.
